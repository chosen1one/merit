What is Merits and Demerits system?

  • The system is an objective way to monitor and track learner behavior.
  • Schools use the merit and demerit record within a point-based system to punish misbehavior or reward good performance.

Why we use this system?

  • Aim to establish discipline and a purposeful school environment dedicated to the accomplishment of the vision and mission of the school.
  • Learners will be accountable for their actions which will attract both positive and negative consequences.
  • Ability to act responsibly, independently of adult supervision is considered the ultimate achievement.

MERIT LEVELS

  • Merit Level 4 (300 points and above) Presenting a “Certificate of Platinum Student”
  • Merit Level 3 (250 – 299 points and above) Presenting a “Certificate of Golden Student”
  • Merit Level 2 (200 – 249 points and above) Presenting a “Certificate of Silver Student”
  • Merit Level 1 (150 – 199 points and above) Presenting a “Certificate of Bronze Student”
  • Standard Level (61 – 149 points) Encouraging to be a well-behaved and successful member of society

DEMERIT LEVELS

  • Demerit Level 1 (51 – 60 points) Talking to the Pastoral Care Head § Oral warning from Pastoral Care Head (3 times)
  • Demerit Level 2 (41 – 50 points) Corrective teaching/counseling § Warning in written form
  • Demerit Level 3 (31 – 40 points) Suspension from any kind of Olympiad and project competitions.§ Informing parents § Warning in written form
  • Demerit Level 4 (21 – 30 points) Letter of notification signed by parents to ensure that parents are informed about all the actions school may take after this point § Warning in written form
